Organic Chemistry

A functional group is an atom or group of atoms responsible for the typical chemical reactions of a molecule.

Distillation: The first step in oil refining. Since boiling points of molecules increase as molecular weight increases, separation is roughly in order of increasing numbers of carbon atoms per molecule. The crude oil mixture is heated, fractions turn into vapour, rise, condense and the liquid is collected. Usually the ‘fraction’ is still a mixture and requires further refining.

Method of separating fractions in crude oil. 1. In a furnace, crude oil is heated until it turns into a vapour. 2. Vapours travel up through the fractionating column. 3. The vapour moves up from the hot bottom to the cooler top. 4. At different heights of the fractionating column, the gas liquifies at each of its levels. 5. A fraction is a group of hydrocarbons with a similar length chain. Similar length chain hydrocarbons have a similar boiling point. The longer the molecule, the stronger the intermolecular force, and will need more energy to turn the liquid into a gas. Fractional Distillation separates multiple fractions with similar boiling point Simple Distillation separates on mixture into two mixtures

Saturated Hydrocarbons:

• Saturated hydrocarbons are hydrocarbons that contain only single bonds between carbon atoms

• Simplest class of hydrocarbons

• Each carbon atom is bonded to as many hydrogen atoms as possible. i.e. the carbon atoms are saturated with hydrogen

• This makes them relatively unreactive, apart from their interaction with oxygen in the air in the case of combustion

Alkanes: • The alkanes are a family of hydrocarbons • Alkanes are saturated hydrocarbons with only single bonds between the carbon atoms • Some can be arranged in continuous straight chains in a branched chain or a ring. Even if you have a L or C shape hydrocarbon, you still count the end carbon for the primary name. Nonane C9H20 Decane C10H22 General formula for alkanes Cn = H2n+2 Isomers are organic molecules with the same molecular formula but a different structural formula Structural isomers have • Different names • Different chemical properties • Different shapes Alkyl groups are types of functional group/attachments When working out the position of an alkyl group you start counting from the end that will give you the smallest number. If you have a double bond then you count from the closest end to the double bond. When naming isomers and its attachments, you name them in alphabetical order.
